Marvel Cinematic Universe

The MCU kicked off with Iron Man in 2008 and has since become the biggest film franchise ever, with dozens of movies and shows all tied together.

Phase One: The Beginning

This is where it all started leading up to the first big team-up.

  • Iron Man (2008)
  • The Incredible Hulk (2008)
  • Iron Man 2 (2010)
  • Thor (2011)
  • Captain America: The First Avenger (2011)
  • The Avengers (2012)

Phase Two: Expanding the Universe

The heroes got deeper storylines and new faces like the Guardians showed up.

  • Iron Man 3 (2013)
  • Thor: The Dark World (2013)
  • Captain America: The Winter Soldier (2014)
  • Guardians of the Galaxy (2014)
  • Avengers: Age of Ultron (2015)
  • Ant-Man (2015)

Phase Three: The Infinity Saga

Everything built to this — the Infinity Saga wrapped up with two massive Avengers movies.

  • Captain America: Civil War (2016)
  • Doctor Strange (2016)
  • Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2 (2017)
  • Spider-Man: Homecoming (2017)
  • Thor: Ragnarok (2017)
  • Black Panther (2018)
  • Avengers: Infinity War (2018)
  • Ant-Man and the Wasp (2018)
  • Captain Marvel (2019)
  • Avengers: Endgame (2019)
  • Spider-Man: Far From Home (2019)

Iconic Characters

Iron Man
Tony Stark — genius, billionaire, guy in the suit. Played by Robert Downey Jr.
Captain America
Steve Rogers, WWII super soldier with a strong moral compass. Played by Chris Evans.
Thor
God of Thunder, swings Mjolnir, from Asgard. Played by Chris Hemsworth.
Black Widow
Natasha Romanoff, ex-assassin turned Avenger. Played by Scarlett Johansson.
Spider-Man
Peter Parker, kid from Queens with spider powers. Played by Tom Holland.

Why Marvel Movies Matter

Love them or not, Marvel proved a shared movie universe could actually work. These films mix fun action with real themes like sacrifice and responsibility, and they changed how Hollywood thinks about franchises.
